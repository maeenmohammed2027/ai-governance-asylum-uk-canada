# Data Governance for Responsible AI in Asylum Decision-Making: UK and Canada

Independent comparative research | Maeen Mohammed | July 2026

## Summary

This study examines how data governance supports the responsible adoption of
AI in asylum decision-making, through a qualitative comparative case study of
the United Kingdom and Canada assessed against the seven characteristics of
trustworthy AI in the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0).

## Key findings

- The two jurisdictions embody distinct governance models: the UK is
  **evaluation-led** (publicly evaluating deployed tools such as Asylum Case
  Summarisation and Facial Age Estimation), while Canada is **governance-led**
  (mandatory Algorithmic Impact Assessments under the Directive on Automated
  Decision-Making).
- Each model manages a different failure mode: evaluation-led governance
  surfaces errors after deployment but makes them visible and correctable;
  governance-led control reduces risk upfront but produces little public
  evidence of operational performance.
- Drawing on the author's 14 years of field experience in refugee registration
  and verification operations, the paper argues that credible governance must
  reach below the policy layer to the operational data layer.
